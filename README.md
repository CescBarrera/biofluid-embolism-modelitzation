# Biophysical Modeling of Gas Microemboli Dynamics in the Bloodstream

This repository contains the theoretical framework, mathematical modeling, and biophysical analysis regarding the radial and translational dynamics of gas bubbles (microemboli) within the human circulatory system.
https://github.com/CescBarrera/biofluid-embolism-modelitzation/blob/main/README.md
## Project Overview

This research explores the hydrodynamic and thermodynamic behavior of gas microembolisms. The study focuses on how boundary conditions, viscous forces, and pressure gradients affect bubble transport, stability, and potential risks in microcirculation.

### Key Physics & Biophysics Concepts Covered:
* **Fluid Mechanics in Biological Systems:** Modeling blood behavior as a viscous fluid and resolving complex hydrodynamic fields.
* **Radial Dynamics:** Investigation of bubble expansion, stabilization, and collapse using the **Rayleigh-Plesset equation** framework.
* **Translational Dynamics:** Evaluation of transport phenomena, including buoyancy, viscous drag forces ($C_d$), and the **Added Mass effect** ($M_a$) in confined geometries.
* **Clinical Applications:** Analyzing the physical parameters that trigger microvascular occlusion and decompression sickness.
